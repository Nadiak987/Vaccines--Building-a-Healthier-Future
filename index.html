Introduction

Vaccines are one of the most effective tools in combating infectious diseases, safeguarding public health, and saving lives worldwide.

Vaccines work by preparing your immune system to fight diseases without exposing you to the full force of the illness.

Key Benefits of Vaccines

  • Disease Prevention: Vaccines have eradicated diseases like smallpox and reduced measles mortality rates globally.
  • Cost-Effective: Vaccines save healthcare costs by preventing hospitalizations.
  • Herd Immunity: Vaccines protect vulnerable populations by reducing disease spread.

Myth-Busting

Myth: Vaccines are unsafe.

Fact: Vaccines undergo rigorous testing for safety and effectiveness before approval.

Myth: Vaccines can cause the diseases they aim to prevent.

Fact: Vaccines contain inactive or weakened forms of viruses that cannot cause the disease.

Timeline of Vaccine Milestones

  • 1796: First smallpox vaccine developed.
  • 1905: Jacobson v. Massachusetts case legitimized vaccine mandates.
  • 1980: Smallpox eradicated globally.

Call to Action

Vaccines save lives. Stay informed, get vaccinated, and protect your community.
